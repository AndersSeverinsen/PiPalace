Halloween Tree Stump

Learn how to make a 3D-printed Halloween Tree Stump LED candle holder.

Introduction

Just in time for Halloween, I made this 3D-printed Halloween Tree Stump LED candle holder. Designed to be easy to print, and creates a unique Halloween decoration, that is not quite like the traditional Jack-O-Lanterns.

You will need:

- A 3D Printer with at minimum a 10x10x10cm printable area

- A spool of wood colored PLA filament

- An LED candle

3D Printing

The Tree Stump was originally designed as a pen holder, by Thingiverse user Daniel Stein (here), but I then remixed it and added space for the LED candle and of course the Halloween themed carved out face.

Designing

I first remixed the pen holder in Autodesk® Tinkercad®, to add a bigger hole that would fit the LED candle. Thereafter I designed the Halloween Pumpkin face in Autodesk® Fusion 360®, and after importing that model to Autodesk® Tinkercad® I could retract the face from the tree stump.

You can get the stl files from the link below or from Thingiverse here.

  • Download .zip

Printing

The part is printed with wood colored filament, with NO support. Though you will need to do some manual clean-up afterwards (See images).

Assembly

Now just turn on the LED candle, and place it inside the stump, and you are finished!
